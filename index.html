The Spotlight of Visual Attention

We can only process a narrow slice of what’s in front of us, while everything outside that focus fades into the background. In data visualization, this means what you emphasize becomes the story your audience remembers. Placement, contrast, and hierarchy guide the eye, while clutter dilutes the message and drains attention. Effective design isn’t about displaying everything at once—it’s about steering focus toward the points that matter most.

The Limits of Working Memory

People can only juggle a few chunks of information at once — usually around 3–5 meaningful units. This well-known bottleneck (from Miller’s Law and later refinements) has big implications for data work. If you present too many numbers or categories at once, you overload your audience and reduce clarity. Good analysis isn’t just about accuracy; it’s about structuring information so the brain can hold onto it long enough to compare, reason, and decide.
